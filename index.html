Step by step

The diagonals of a rhombus are perpendicular bisectors of each other. They divide the rhombus into 4 right-angled triangles that together fill exactly half of the d₁ × d₂ bounding rectangle — hence Area = ½ × d₁ × d₂. This formula works for all rhombuses and also for squares (where d₁ = d₂).
